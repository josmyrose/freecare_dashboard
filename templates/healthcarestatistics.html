Healthcare Access Improvement Outcomes

1. More People Sought Healthcare

After removing fees, 23% more mothers and children visited health facilities when sick
Biggest increases were for preventive care like vaccinations and checkups

2. Better Health Outcomes

Diarrhea cases dropped by 15% in areas with free healthcare
More children received complete vaccinations (up from 68% to 82%)
Fewer severe malnutrition cases were reported

3. Helped Poor Families Most

Lowest-income families benefited twice as much as wealthier families
Removed financial barriers that previously prevented care-seeking

4. Quality Remained Good

No evidence that free services became overcrowded or lower quality
Patient satisfaction scores stayed the same or improved slightly
