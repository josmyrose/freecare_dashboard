🏥 Project Overview: Impact of Free Primary Care on Healthcare Utilization Using Machine Learning

Access to affordable healthcare plays a vital role in improving health outcomes, especially for low-income families. This project investigates how providing free primary healthcare influences when and where mothers seek medical care for their children.

Using data from a randomized controlled trial (RCT)—where households received different healthcare interventions (free primary care, health worker visits, or no intervention)—we explore the behavioral shifts in healthcare-seeking decisions.

To quantify this impact, the project combines:

  • 🔍 Machine Learning Models: Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, and XGBoost to classify healthcare utilization patterns.
  • 📊 Causal Inference Techniques: Including Difference-in-Differences and Propensity Score Matching to estimate the true effect of removing user fees.

The findings offer data-driven insights for policymakers, helping them design effective interventions that enhance access to care, especially for vulnerable communities.
